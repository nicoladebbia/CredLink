# Sub-processor Change Playbook

## Change Management Process

### 1. Pre-Announcement (Internal)
- **Due Diligence**: Vet new sub-processor for security, compliance, data protection
- **Risk Assessment**: Evaluate data protection risks and transfer mechanisms
- **Contract Review**: Ensure sub-processor agreement meets DPA requirements
- **Approval**: Internal legal and security team approval

### 2. Customer Notice (30 Days)
- **Public List Update**: Add new sub-processor to public disclosure
- **Email Notification**: Send to all customers with DPA
- **Dashboard Alert**: In-app notification
- **RSS Feed**: Automated feed update

### 3. Objection Window (30 Days)
- **Monitor**: Track customer objections
- **Respond**: Acknowledge objections within 2 business days
- **Negotiate**: Work with customer to address concerns
- **Mitigate**: Implement additional safeguards if reasonable
- **Terminate Option**: Allow termination for affected processing if unresolved

### 4. Enablement
- **After 30 Days**: Enable sub-processor if no unresolved objections
- **Urgent Cases**: Immediate enablement with retroactive notice (security/compliance only)
- **Documentation**: Update all DPA annexes and sub-processor lists

## Objection Handling

### Reasonable Grounds
- Data residency concerns
- Security certification gaps
- Transfer mechanism inadequacy
- Regulatory compliance issues

### Resolution Options
1. Additional contractual safeguards
2. Alternative sub-processor
3. Enhanced monitoring/auditing
4. Termination for affected processing

## Communication Templates

**New Sub-processor Notice**:
"We are adding [Name] as a sub-processor for [Purpose]. Details: [Link]. Object within 30 days if reasonable data protection grounds exist."

**Objection Response**:
"We acknowledge your objection to [Sub-processor]. Let's discuss mitigation options within 5 business days."
