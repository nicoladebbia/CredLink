# Privacy & Data Protection

## Overview

We are committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of our customers and end-users. Our privacy practices comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy regulations worldwide.

## Data Protection Principles

### Core Principles

**Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency**
- Clear privacy notices at point of collection
- Transparent processing purposes
- Lawful basis for all data processing
- No deceptive or hidden data practices

**Purpose Limitation**
- Data collected for specified, legitimate purposes
- No further processing incompatible with original purpose
- Clear documentation of processing purposes
- Regular purpose alignment reviews

**Data Minimization**
- Collect only data necessary for stated purposes
- Avoid excessive or irrelevant data collection
- Regular data inventory and necessity assessments
- Automated data minimization controls

**Accuracy**
- Mechanisms to update or correct personal data
- Data validation at point of collection
- Regular data quality checks
- User-facing correction interfaces

**Storage Limitation**
- Data retained only as long as necessary
- Automated deletion after retention period
- Clear retention schedules per data category
- Secure deletion procedures

**Integrity and Confidentiality**
- Technical and organizational security measures
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls and audit logging
- Regular security assessments

**Accountability**
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
- Privacy by design and default
- Data processing records maintained
- Compliance monitoring and reporting

## Data Subject Rights

### Rights Under GDPR

**Right to Access**
- Request copy of personal data
- Response time: 30 days (extendable to 60 days)
- Format: Machine-readable (JSON/CSV)
- Self-service: Available through dashboard

**Right to Rectification**
- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete data
- Response time: 30 days
- Self-service: Available for account data

**Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)**
- Delete personal data when no longer necessary
- Delete when consent withdrawn
- Delete when objection raised
- Exceptions: Legal obligations, public interest

**Right to Restrict Processing**
- Temporarily restrict processing during disputes
- Mark data as restricted
- Limited processing (storage only)
- Notification of restrictions

**Right to Data Portability**
- Receive personal data in structured format
- Transmit data to another controller
- Format: JSON, CSV, or API
- Self-service export available

**Right to Object**
- Object to processing based on legitimate interest
- Object to direct marketing (always honored)
- Object to automated decision-making
- Assessment within 30 days

**Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making**
- Right to human intervention
- Right to contest decision
- Right to explanation
- Currently: No solely automated decisions made

### Rights Under CCPA

**Right to Know**
- Categories of personal information collected
- Sources of personal information
- Business purpose for collection
- Third parties with whom data is shared

**Right to Delete**
- Request deletion of personal information
- Exceptions: Necessary for business operations
- Verification required
- Response within 45 days

**Right to Opt-Out of Sale**
- We do not sell personal information
- No opt-out required
- Clear notice maintained

**Right to Non-Discrimination**
- No discrimination for exercising rights
- No denial of service
- No different pricing or service levels

## International Data Transfers

### Transfer Mechanisms

**Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**
- EU Commission approved SCCs (2021 version)
- Controller-to-processor and processor-to-processor
- Supplementary measures implemented
- Available upon request

**Adequacy Decisions**
- Transfers to countries with adequacy decisions
- Current: EEA, UK, Switzerland, Canada (commercial)
- Monitoring of adequacy status changes
- Contingency plans for adequacy withdrawals

**Data Localization**
- Regional data residency options available
- EU data can remain in EU region
- Customer choice for data location
- Geographic controls in custody service

### Transfer Impact Assessment

- Conducted for all third-country transfers
- Evaluated local laws and access rights
- Supplementary safeguards implemented
- Regular reassessment (annually)

## Privacy by Design

### Technical Measures

**Data Minimization**
- Collect only necessary fields
- Progressive disclosure in forms
- Automated data purging
- Anonymization where possible

**Pseudonymization**
- User IDs instead of names in logs
- Hashed identifiers for analytics
- Separation of identifying and behavioral data
- Encryption of personal data at rest

**Access Controls**
- Role-based access to personal data
- Least privilege principle
- MFA for access to sensitive data
- Audit logging of all access

**Encryption**
- AES-256 for data at rest
- TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- End-to-end encryption for sensitive fields
- Key management with rotation

### Organizational Measures

**Privacy Training**
- Mandatory privacy training for all employees
- Annual refresher training
- Role-specific privacy training
- GDPR and CCPA awareness

**Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**
- Required for high-risk processing
- Assessment of necessity and proportionality
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Consultation with DPO when required

**Privacy Policies**
- Clear, transparent privacy notice
- Updated with any processing changes
- Version control and change log
- Plain language explanations

**Vendor Management**
- Privacy assessment of all vendors
- DPAs with all processors
- Regular vendor privacy reviews
- Vendor breach notification requirements
