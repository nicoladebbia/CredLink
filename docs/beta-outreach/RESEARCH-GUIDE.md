## Finding Companies

### Research Sources

**1. Industry Lists:**
- Crunchbase (filter by industry)
- AngelList (startups)
- G2 (software categories)
- Capterra (by use case)

**2. News & Media:**
- TechCrunch (funding announcements)
- Industry publications
- Conference attendee lists
- Award winners

**3. Direct Search:**
- Google: "[industry] + companies"
- LinkedIn company search
- Industry associations
- Trade show exhibitors

**4. Competitor Analysis:**
- Who uses similar tools?
- Who has the problem we solve?
- Who's mentioned in case studies?

### Qualification Questions

Before adding to list:
- [ ] Do they handle images at scale?
- [ ] Is authenticity important to them?
- [ ] Do they have technical resources?
- [ ] Can they afford a paid solution?
- [ ] Are they growing/innovative?

## Email Discovery

### Free Methods

**1. Email Pattern Guessing:**

Common patterns:
- `firstname@company.com`
- `firstname.lastname@company.com`
- `firstnamelastname@company.com`
- `f.lastname@company.com`
- `flastname@company.com`

Verify with:
- Email verification tools (free tier)
- LinkedIn messaging (see if they respond)

**2. Company Website:**
- Contact page
- Team bios
- Press contacts
- Support emails (then ask for right person)

**3. LinkedIn:**
- Some profiles show email
- InMail (if you have premium)
- Mutual connections

**4. Google Search:**
```
"[Name]" "[Company]" email
"[Name]" "@[company domain]"
site:github.com "[Name]" "[Company]"
```

## Qualification Criteria

### Must Have:
- [ ] Company handles images (photos, graphics, etc.)
- [ ] Authenticity/trust is important to their business
- [ ] Technical team exists (can integrate API)
- [ ] Company is active/growing (not dying)

### Nice to Have:
- [ ] High image volume (>1,000/month)
- [ ] Recent funding or revenue growth
- [ ] Known for innovation
- [ ] Existing tech stack (APIs, cloud)
- [ ] Pain point is visible (fraud, trust issues)

- ‚ùå No technical team
- ‚ùå Company struggling/laying off
- ‚ùå Very small image volume
- ‚ùå No budget for tools

## Research Template

For each prospect, collect:

### Basic Info
- **Name:** Full name
- **Company:** Company name
- **Email:** Work email
- **Role:** Job title
- **Industry:** Primary industry

### Context
- **Use Case:** How they'd use CredLink
- **Priority:** High/Medium/Low
- **Status:** Not Contacted / Contacted / Responded / Qualified / Rejected
- **Notes:** Any relevant info
- **Source:** Where you found them

### Tracking
- **Contact Date:** When you reached out
- **Follow Up Date:** When to follow up
- **Response:** Their reply (if any)

## Success Metrics

### Quality over Quantity

**Good List:**
- 100 prospects
- 80% verified emails
- 60% high priority
- 100% qualified (meet criteria)
- Detailed notes on all

**Poor List:**
- 200 prospects
- 30% verified emails
- 20% high priority
- 50% qualified
- Minimal notes

**Aim for quality.** Better to have 50 perfect prospects than 200 mediocre ones.
