### Scoring Categories (Total 50 Points)

**1. Industry Fit (15 Points)**
```yaml
News Organizations: 15 points
  - Highest pain severity (deepfakes, misinformation)
  - Budget availability (trust & safety budgets)
  - Regulatory pressure (EU DSA compliance)
  - Clear ROI (credibility, cost savings)

Stock Photo Agencies: 14 points
  - Strong competitive differentiation need
  - Creator protection requirements
  - Buyer trust and pricing power
  - AI competition pressure

E-commerce Platforms: 13 points
  - Counterfeit detection critical
  - Customer trust impact on revenue
  - Brand protection high priority
  - Scalable use case

Creator Platforms: 12 points
  - IP protection increasingly important
  - Creator retention and trust
  - Content ownership disputes
  - Growing market need

Research Institutions: 10 points
  - Academic integrity important
  - Funding agency requirements
  - Smaller budgets typically
  - Longer sales cycles

Other Industries: 5 points
  - Variable fit and priority
  - May require custom solutions
  - Higher support requirements
  - Unclear market size
```

**2. Priority Level (15 Points)**
```yaml
Critical: 15 points
  - "Core to our business"
  - Immediate need, budget allocated
  - Decision maker engaged
  - High urgency, quick timeline

Important: 10 points
  - "Significant business impact"
  - Clear need, budget available
  - Decision process in progress
  - Moderate urgency

Nice-to-have: 5 points
  - "Would improve operations"
  - Interest but no urgency
  - Budget may be available
  - Lower priority

Exploring: 1 point
  - "Not sure yet"
  - Early stage research
  - Budget uncertain
  - Long timeline
```

**3. Volume Estimate (10 Points)**
```yaml
100k+ items/month: 10 points
  - Enterprise scale usage
  - Significant revenue potential
  - Technical capability confirmed
  - Long-term value high

50k-100k items/month: 8 points
  - Strong business case
  - Good revenue potential
  - Technical capability likely
  - Solid long-term value

10k-50k items/month: 6 points
  - Moderate business case
  - Some revenue potential
  - Technical capability possible
  - Decent long-term value

1k-10k items/month: 3 points
  - Small business case
  - Limited revenue potential
  - Technical capability uncertain
  - Lower long-term value
```

**4. Technical Capability (5 Points)**
```yaml
API Integration: 5 points
  - Can integrate independently
  - Development resources available
  - Quick time to value
  - Lower support overhead

SDK Integration: 4 points
  - Prefers SDK but can code
  - Some development resources
  - Reasonable time to value
  - Moderate support overhead

Webhook Support: 3 points
  - Technical but needs integration help
  - Limited development resources
  - Longer time to value
  - Higher support overhead

Limited Resources: 2 points
  - Needs significant help
  - Minimal development resources
  - Longest time to value
  - Highest support overhead
```

**5. Use Case Quality (5 Points)**
```yaml
Detailed & Specific (200+ words): 5 points
  - Clear understanding of problem
  - Specific use case identified
  - Success metrics defined
  - Implementation considered

Good Detail (100-200 words): 4 points
  - Good problem understanding
  - Reasonable use case
  - Some success metrics
  - Basic implementation plan

Basic Detail (50-100 words): 3 points
  - Basic problem statement
  - General use case
  - Limited metrics
  - Implementation unclear

Minimal Detail (<50 words): 1 point
  - Vague problem statement
  - Unclear use case
  - No metrics defined
  - No implementation plan
```

## ðŸ“‹ Review Process Workflow

### Step 1: Initial Screening (Day 1)

**Automated Checks:**
```yaml
Completeness Check:
- All required fields filled
- Valid email format
- Company website exists
- Phone number valid
- Industry not "other" without explanation

Quality Check:
- Use case length >10 words
- Priority level selected
- Technical capability selected
- Beta requirements agreed

Spam Check:
- No suspicious email domains
- No duplicate applications
- No competitor signatures
- No nonsense content
```

**Manual Review:**
```yaml
Initial Assessment:
- Business legitimacy verification
- Contact person role confirmation
- Company size and revenue estimation
- Industry pain point validation
- Technical capability assessment

Scoring:
- Apply scoring framework
- Calculate total score
- Assign priority ranking
- Flag for detailed review
```

### Step 2: Detailed Review (Day 2)

**High-Priority Applications (Score 35+):**
```yaml
Review Team: Founder + CTO
Research Required:
- Company website and recent news
- LinkedIn profiles of contacts
- Industry trends and competitors
- Technical stack assessment
- Budget and authority verification

Evaluation Criteria:
- Strategic fit with CredLink roadmap
- Reference customer potential
- Case study opportunities
- Partnership possibilities
- Long-term value assessment

Decision: Fast-track to acceptance or rejection
```

**Medium-Priority Applications (Score 25-34):**
```yaml
Review Team: Sales Lead + Engineer
Research Required:
- Basic company verification
- Contact role confirmation
- Use case feasibility
- Technical integration complexity

Evaluation Criteria:
- Resource requirements
- Success probability
- Support needs
- Expansion potential

Decision: Accept, waitlist, or reject
```

**Low-Priority Applications (Score <25):**
```yaml
Review Team: Sales Rep
Research Required:
- Basic verification only
- Obvious disqualifiers check

Evaluation Criteria:
- Minimum requirements met
- Resource availability
- Strategic value

Decision: Waitlist or reject
```

### Step 3: Selection Meeting (Day 3)

**Attendees:**
- Founder (Product & Strategy)
- CTO (Technical Evaluation)
- Sales Lead (Commercial Assessment)
- Customer Success (Support Requirements)

**Agenda:**
1. Review application pool and scoring results
2. Discuss high-priority applications and concerns
3. Evaluate diversity requirements
4. Assess resource capacity for onboarding
5. Make final selection decisions
6. Plan communication strategy

**Decision Framework:**
```yaml
Accept Criteria:
âœ… Score 30+ OR
âœ… Score 25+ with strategic value AND
âœ… Meets diversity requirements AND
âœ… Resource capacity available

Waitlist Criteria:
âœ… Score 25-29 but capacity full OR
âœ… Score 20-24 with strong potential

Reject Criteria:
âŒ Score <20 OR
âŒ Missing requirements OR
âŒ Resource constraints too high
```
