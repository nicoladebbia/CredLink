2. What Are Cookies?

Definition

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember your actions and preferences over time.

How Cookies Work

  • When you visit our website, we send cookies to your device
  • Your browser stores these cookies locally
  • On subsequent visits, your browser sends these cookies back to us
  • This allows us to recognize your device and remember your preferences

Types of Cookie Technologies

  • HTTP Cookies: Traditional web cookies for browser-based tracking
  • Local Storage: Browser storage for larger amounts of data
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage that clears when the browser closes
  • Web Beacons: Small transparent images for tracking and analytics

7. Cookie Lifetime

Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. We use these for:

  • Maintaining your login session
  • CSRF protection
  • Temporary UI state

Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a specified period or until you delete them. We use these for:

  • Remembering your preferences (1 year)
  • API authentication (30 days)
  • Analytics and tracking (2 years)
  • Cookie consent (1 year)

Cookie Expiration

Cookies automatically expire after their designated lifetime. You can also manually delete cookies through:

  • Your browser's cookie settings
  • Our cookie settings panel
  • Clearing browser data

üîÑ Automatic Renewal

Some persistent cookies may be automatically renewed when you visit our website, extending their lifetime from the date of your last visit.
